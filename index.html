Covid 19 Impact Analysis
A View On Nigeria

Between late 2019 and early 2021, COVID-19 swept from its origins in Wuhan, China, to Nigeria's shores, arriving on February 27, 2020, when an Italian national tested positive in Lagos. What followed was a data story of lockdowns, economic hardship, and human resilience — tracked meticulously through Excel dashboards, pivot tables, and trend charts. Cases surged in waves, Lagos bore the heaviest burden, and over 40% of Nigerians faced economic devastation as daily incomes vanished overnight. Yet despite being Africa's 5th most affected country, Nigeria's relatively young population and prior outbreak experience kept mortality figures surprisingly low. By March 2021, the arrival of the first AstraZeneca vaccine doses signaled a turning point — and when the final dashboard was assembled, the numbers told not just a story of infection and loss, but one of policy, inequality, and a nation's extraordinary will to endure.
